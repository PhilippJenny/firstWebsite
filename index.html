Chess: The "Kalashnikov" Sicilian

When does it come to play

The "Kalashnikov" variation in the Sicilian comes into play when black plays: 2. ... Nc6, followed by 3. d4 cxd4 4. Kxd4 e5!?. The immediate e5 completes the transition to the "Kalashnikov" variation.

Relevance at top level play

While the "Kalashnikov" Sicilian is not part of the main repertoire of top-level players, it still appears quite often as it's a sound opening.

Characteristics

The Sicilian opening is quite tactics dependent, but the Kalashnikov variation lies the focus more on positional aspects
